起源 · Origins

Where grasslands
meet the sky

呼和浩特. The Blue City. Capital of Inner Mongolia — where the Mongolian steppe stretches to every horizon and winters arrive with a sharpness that reminds you exactly where you are.

Huihao Fan didn't grow up in the city's heart. He grew up on its rural edge — in a 广场, a small plaza, a cluster of low-rise buildings surrounded on all sides by farmland. Neighbors knew each other by name. Seasons were marked not by calendar but by the color of the fields.

It was a childhood of open skies, communal courtyards, and the patience that only comes from living somewhere nothing moves fast. A patience that would one day define a meticulous scientific career.

广场 · The Plaza

A childhood drawn in dust and light

Hover the glowing spots to unlock memories from the plaza.

冬日

Winter Mornings

The courtyard froze overnight. Children slid across it in cloth shoes before school, breath rising in grey clouds. Coal smoke and steamed buns filled the cold air. Every winter morning felt the same — and that sameness was its own kind of warmth.

麦田

The Wheat Fields

Beyond the last building, the wheat began. In summer it turned gold — rows stretching to the horizon, moving like slow water in the wind. Years later, facing enormous datasets, Huihao would feel the same thing: the pleasure of finding pattern in something vast.

夜空

Nights Without Light Pollution

Far from city lights, the night sky over the rural plaza was enormous. The Milky Way stretched overhead on clear nights. Summer evenings were spent lying in the courtyard, counting stars — the first encounter with something beautiful, infinite, and impossible to fully enumerate.

集市

The Weekly Market

Once a week, vendors from nearby villages gathered near the plaza. Vegetables, fabric, dried goods, noise, color. Against the usual quiet, it felt like the whole world had arrived. It was the village's heartbeat — and it taught, without words, that data always has a story behind it.
